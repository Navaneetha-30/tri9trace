# CardioTrack CT-200 Operator Manual

## Introduction

The CardioTrack CT-200 is a portable cardiac monitoring device intended for
clinical use. This manual describes installation, operation, and maintenance.

### Intended Use

The CT-200 is intended for non-invasive monitoring of adult patients in
hospital and clinic settings. It is not intended for home use.

### Warnings

Do not operate the device near an open flame. Ensure the power cable is
undamaged before each use. Patients with pacemakers must be monitored by
trained personnel only.

## Device Overview

The CT-200 consists of a display unit, a sensor array, and a power module.

#### System Components

### Display

The 7-inch colour display shows real-time ECG, heart rate, and alarm status.
Backlight default brightness is 60%.

## Installation

Place the device on a stable surface within 1.5 m of a grounded outlet.

### Power Requirements

Input: 100-240 VAC, 50/60 Hz. Fuse rating: 2 A slow-blow.

### Power Requirements

An uninterruptible power supply is recommended for continuous monitoring.
Battery backup provides up to 4 hours of operation.

## Operating Instructions

### Starting a Session

Press and hold the power button for 2 seconds. Wait for the self-test to
complete before attaching sensors.

### Recording

Recording starts when a session begins. All recordings are stored on the
internal drive. Recordings are saved in CT-REC format.

### Export

To export a recording, connect a USB drive and select Export from the
recording menu. Exported files are saved in CT-REC format.

## Alarm Management

This section covers alarm configuration and behaviour.

### Alarms

Alarm conditions include: heart rate above 120 bpm or below 40 bpm, lead
fault, and low battery. Alarms are audible and visual. Alarm volume is
adjustable from the settings menu.

## Maintenance

### Maintenance Procedures

Clean the exterior with a soft cloth dampened with isopropyl alcohol. Do
not immerse the device in liquid. Calibrate the sensor array every 12 months
using the CT-CAL calibration kit. Record the calibration date on the
service log.

## Troubleshooting and FAQ

Common issues: a blank display usually means a loose power connection or a
blown fuse; erratic heart-rate readings typically point to a worn patient
cable. The device is not MR-conditional and must not be used during MRI.

## Safety Limits

The device enforces a hard heart-rate alarm threshold of 140 bpm. Patient
load must not exceed 7 kg applied to the sensor array. Operation outside
10-40 C voids the warranty.

## Glossary

ECG: electrocardiogram. BPM: beats per minute. Lead fault: loss of electrical
contact with the patient.
